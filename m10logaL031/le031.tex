	\noindent{\bf Task 2.}\\
	It is an exponential decrease; the growth factor $a$ is:
	$$a=\left( 1 - {1.5\over 100}\right)=0.985$$
	The decay equation is therefore:
	$$N(t)=140 \cdot 0.985^t$$
	For the task, we set the number of active cores to 100% at the beginning and at the end
	of the observation period to 42% and get:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		42\%&=&100\% \cdot 0.985^t & | : 100\%\\
		0.42 &=& 0.985^t &| \lg()\\
		\lg 0.42 &=& t \cdot \lg 0.985 &| : \lg 0.985\\
		t &=& {\lg 0.42\over \lg 0.985} &= 57.4\\
	\end{array}
	$$
	Answer: after $57.4~s$ the number of active cores is still $42\%$ of the initial value.

	\noindent {\bf Task 15.}\\
	The functional term for type A bacteria is (t = hours):
	$$y_A=14000 \cdot 2^t$$
	Type B bacteria multiply by this factor
	$$a=1+{p\over 100} = 2.3$$
	half hourly, so the function term is:
	$$y_B=5000 \cdot 2.3^{2t}$$
	We equate both terms and solve for t:
	$$
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		14000 \cdot 2^t &=& 5000 \cdot 2.3^{2t} &| : 5000 \qquad :2^t \\
		{14000 \over 5000} &=& \frac{2.3^{2t}}{2^t} \\
		2.8 &=& \left( \frac{2.3^2}{2} \right)^t &| \log \\
		\log 2.8 &=& t\cdot \log{2.645} &| : \log{2,645} \\
		t&=& 1.06
	\end{array}
	$$
	After about an hour, both bacterial cultures are equal.
