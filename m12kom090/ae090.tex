	{\bf Combinatorics, some high school exams } - {\it The suggested solutions come from our own calculations }
	
	\begin{enumerate}[1.]
		{\bf \item There are 10 guests at a New Year's Eve party}. At 12 o'clock there will be a toast with champagne, with everyone toasting each other once. How often do the glasses ring? [45]
		{\bf \item How many possible direct flight connections } are there between the capitals of all 28 EU countries? [378]
		{\bf \item Three dogs are photographed in the animal shelter:} a husky, a Jack-Russel and a bulldog. The three photos will then be published side by side on the website.
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How many arrangements of the photos are possible? [6]
			\item How many options are there if each dog has either their eyes closed or open? [48]
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item Lunerald found a suitcase of money. Unfortunately it is locked with two three-digit combination locks.}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How many combinations are there to try? [2000]
			\item How many combinations are there if the six digits are a date of birth (DD.MM.YY {\it for simplicity there are no leap years})? [36500]
		\end{enumerate}
		{\bf \item In a television show} the 7 candidates, 4 women and 3 men, sit in a row. How many seating arrangements are there if a distinction is made in terms of people and
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item the two marginal positions should be occupied by men, [720]
			\item should men and women always alternate in line? [144]
		\end{enumerate}
		{\bf \item A game is played with a “lucky wall”.} This consists of 20 fields onto which the number 200 is randomly distributed five times, the number 500 four times and the number 1000 three times. The remaining fields remain empty. How many such distributions are there? [3491888400]
		{\bf \item 12 new employees are to be hired in a department store due to extended closing times }.
		There are 5 positions to be filled in Department A and 7 positions in Department B. 8 people apply for department A and 10 people for department B. How many opportunities are there to fill the vacancies if the positions are within each department
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item cannot be distinguished, [56; 120]
			\item be considered different? [6720; 604800]
			\suspend{enumerate}
			During the welcome, the 12 new employees, 8 women and 4 men, sit in two rows of 6 chairs each. How many seating arrangements are there if there is only a distinction between women and men, and
			\resume{enumerate}
			\item two men sit in each row, [225]
			\item the 4 men sitting next to each other? [6]
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item A gardener plants 10 tulip bulbs} of the red-flowering variety and 10 bulbs of the yellow-flowering variety in two rows of 8 and 12 bulbs. How many planting options are there if there are to be exactly 4 bulbs of the yellow-flowering tulip variety in one of the rows? [78540]
		{\bf \item At the turn of the millennium, the company boss, an amateur mathematician,} organized a prize puzzle among her employees. Answer the two questions asked there:
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item In how many ways can you arrange the prime factors in the prime factor representation of the number 2000? [35]
			\item How many different divisors does the number 2000 have? [20]
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item Vroni invited 3 friends and 4 friends to her birthday party}.
		For a pantomime, the 8 young people are randomly formed
		4 selected.
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How many group options are there if Vroni and
			Peter should be in the selected group together? [15]
			\suspend{enumerate}
			
			
			Only couples of one girl each dance on the dance floor
			a boy. How many different combinations of couples
			on the dance floor there is if
			\resume{enumerate}
			\item all 8 participants in the party dance along, [24]
			\item Of the young men, only Max and Peter dance? [12]
		\end{enumerate}
		
		\newpage
		{\bf \item A presenter invites three politicians to his talk show}, a journalist and
		two members of a citizens' initiative. There is one for the discussion group
		Semi-circular seating arrangement is provided, according to the people
		is differentiated and the moderator takes the middle place.
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item Calculate the number of possible seating arrangements if no other restrictions are taken into account. [720]
			\item The broadcaster has specified that immediately next to the presenter
			on one side the journalist and
			nd on the other side one of the politicians
			should sit. Calculate taking this further restriction into account
			the number of possible seating arrangements. [144]
			\end{enumerate}
			
			{\bf \item The Theater and Film course at a high school} runs the stage version of a
			novels.
			The school auditorium is set up with seating for the premiere; be in the front row
			eight places reserved for guests of honor. Determine the number of
			Opportunities that the five guests of honor who have appeared have to rely on the reserved ones
			to distribute places if
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item the people are not distinguished; [56]
			\item the people are distinguished. [6720]
			\end{enumerate}
			
			{\bf \item Twelve young bands} from all over the world take part in a music competition
			Germany part. Exactly two of them come from Bavaria. Half of the bands only sing songs
			with English lyrics, the other exclusively with German songs
			Texting. In the first round of the competition, the twelve bands perform one after the other
			with one song each; the order of appearances will be drawn.
			There will be a break after the first six performances.
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How many options are there for the order of appearances? [479001600]
			\item How many options are there for the order of appearances,
			if a distinction is only made based on whether a band comes from Bavaria
			or not? [66]
			\item Calculate the probabilities of the following events.
			\begin{enumerate}[A]
				\item "The two Bavarian bands perform before the break." [0.227]
				\item "The two Bavarian bands perform one after the other before the break." [0.07575]
				\item “Bands singing German and English perform alternately.” [0.00217]
			\end{enumerate}
			\end{enumerate}
			
			{\bf \item Eight girls and six boys take part in a P seminar}, including
			Anna and Tobias. The participants will be selected by lottery for a presentation
			and participants put together a team of four people.
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item For each of the following events, specify an term with which
			the probability of the event can be calculated.
			\begin{enumerate}[A]
				\item "Anna and Tobias are part of the team." [0.066]
				\item "The team consists of an equal number of girls and boys." [0.420]
			\end{enumerate}
			\item Describe an event and its probability in a factual context
			can be calculated by the following term: $ {14 \choose 4} - {6 \choose 4} \over {14 \choose 4}$
			\end{enumerate}
			{\bf \item A roller coaster train consists of 12 cars,} four of which are red, five yellow and three blue.
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How many different train combinations are possible? [27720]
			\item How many different car rows are possible if the three blue cars drive directly behind each other? [1260]
			\end{enumerate}
			
			{\bf \item Class 7a of a high school goes to ski camp.} All 11 boys and everyone
			18 girls in the class take part in the week-long ski course.
			The accommodation for the 18 girls in the class has a six-bed,
			a five-bed, a four-bed and a three-bed room available.
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How many different options are there, the girls on the four
			Allocate rooms so that every room is fully occupied? [514594080]
			\suspend{enumerate}
			During a downhill ski run for advanced skiers
			Skiers in class 7a will receive start numbers from 1 to 20 at random
			drawn by the 20 participants. Among them are the 3 girls
			the triple room.
			What is the probability that these 3 girls
			\resume{enumerate}
			\item are among the first ten starters? [0.105]
			\item draw consecutive start numbers? [0.00263]
			\end{enumerate}
			
			
			
			
		\end{enumerate}
