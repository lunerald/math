	{\bf Triangular geometry and centric stretching}
	\begin{enumerate}[1.]
		
		{\bf \item Given is the triangle $ABC$ with $ A(1|1) $, $ B(10|-2) $ and $ C(7|4)$}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			%Task 1
			\item Draw the triangle in a suitable coordinate system. \\
			$ ( 0 \le x \le 11, \quad -3 \le y \le 5 ) $
			
			\item Show mathematically that it is an isosceles triangle
			
			\item Describe in words how to calculate the coordinates of the circumcenter.
			
			\item The circumcenter is $ M(5.5|-0.5) $. Calculate the radius of the circumference
			
			\item By what factor is the area of the circumference greater than the area of the
			triangle?
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item Are the following statements true or false? }
		
		For a centric stretch with the center $ Z $ and the stretch factor $ k $ the following applies:
		
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item If the image point lies between $ Z $ and the original point, $ k $ is negative.
			\item If the image point lies between $ Z $ and the original point, $ 0 < k < 1 $ applies.
			\item Every line through $ Z $ is a fixed line
			\item It is a congruence map for $ k \neq -1 $ and $ k \neq 1 $
		\end{enumerate}
		
		{\bf \item Given is the line $ g: \quad y = 0.75x + 2 $ and $ P(2|3.5) \in g $ .}
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item The straight line is mapped to $ g' $ by centric stretching at the center $ Z(2|2) $ and with the stretching factor $ k = 3 $. Plot $ g $ and $ g' $ in a coordinate system. $ ( 0 \le x \le 7 , \quad 0 \le y \le 7 ) $
			\item Calculate the length of the line $ \overline{ZP'} $
			\item Then calculate the equation of the line $ g' $
			\item The points $ A(4|2.5) $, $ B(6.5|2.5) $ and $ C(4|y_c) $ define the triangle ABC. $ C $ lies on the line $ g $ . Draw the triangle in the coordinate system and calculate its area in a comprehensible way. What particular triangle is this?
			\item The triangle $ ABC $ is stretched centrally at the center $ S(0|0) $ with $ k = -2 $. Calculate the area of triangle $A'B'C'$ .
		\end{enumerate}
		
		%Task 4
		{\bf \item The base area of a truncated pyramid is nine times larger than the top area, its height is $\SI{6.0}{\centi\meter}$.} Calculate the height of the pyramid top above the base area.
		
	\end{enumerate}
