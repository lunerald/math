	\noindent{\bf Task 1. a)}\\
	Let the number of handballs be $x$. Then the number of soccer balls is $2x$ and that of volleyballs
	$x+4$. The 12 tennis balls cost $12\cdot$ \EUR{6} = \EUR{72}. The total costs are:
	$$
	\begin{array}{rcl}
		K(x)&=&(x\cdot 30+2x \cdot 35 +(x+4)\cdot 20 +72)\,\textnormal{\EUR{}}\\
		&=& (30x +70x +20x + 80 + 72)\, \textnormal{\EUR{}}\\
		&=& (120x + 152)\, \textnormal{\EUR{}}
	\end{array}
	$$
	\vspace{0.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 1. b)}\\
	Now we set the variable x = 10:
	$$K(10)=120\cdot 10 + 152 = \textnormal{\EUR{1352}}$$
	The total cost of the balls is \EUR{1352}.
