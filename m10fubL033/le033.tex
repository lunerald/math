	\noindent{\bf Task 1. a)}\\
	
	We substitute $x=3$ into the function term and set it equal to zero:
	$$

	\noindent {\bf task 1. d)}\\
	The highest occurring power, here $x^3$, uses the sign of its prefactor to decide where the function goes for large $|x|$. The prefactor is positive and the power is odd. For this reason:
	$$\lim\limits_{x \to \infty} 0.2 x^3 -1.4 x^2 - 1.8 x +12.6 = \infty$$
	$$\lim\limits_{x \to -\infty} 0.2 x^3 -1.4 x^2 - 1.8 x +12.6 = -\infty$$

	\noindent{\bf Task 3. a)}\\
	Gaps in definition are zeros of the denominator. Using Vieta's theorem we get:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		x^2+2x-3 & = & 0 & = (x+a)(x+b)\\
		a + b & = & 2 \\
		a \cdot b & = & -3 \\
		\Rightarrow a & = & 3 \\
		b & = & -1 \\
		x^2+2x-3 & = & 0 & = (x+3)(x-1)
	\end{array}
	$$
	So the roots of this term are $x_1=-3$ and $x_2=1$.
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 3. b)}\\
	We guess the first zero of the counter, it is $x=1$. We then carry out the polynomial division:
	$$
	\polyset{style=C,div=:,vars=x}
	\polylongdiv{2x^3-2x^2-8x+8}{x-1}
	$$
	We further factor:
	$$2x^2-8 = 2(x^2-4) = 2(x+2)(x-2)$$
	
	This gives us:
	$$f(x)={2(x+2)(x-2)(x-1) \over (x+3)(x-1)}$$
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 3. c)}\\
	We reduce the term $(x-1)$ from the numerator and denominator and look at the behavior of the function in the vicinity of $x=1$:
	$$\lim\limits_{x\to 1^+} {2x^2-8 \over x+3} = -{6 \over 4} = -1.5 $$
	$$\lim\limits_{x\to 1^-} {2x^2-8 \over x+3} = -{6 \over 4} = -1.5 $$
	The limit exists and the function approaches the same point from both sides $y=-1.5$, so the function $f$ can be continued continuously at its definition gap $x=1$.
	
	\noindent{\bf Task 3. d)}\\
	The position $x=-3$ comes into question as a pole position. We also use the limit here:
	$$\lim\limits_{x\to -3^+} {2x^2-8 \over x+3} = "{10 \over +0}" = + \infty $$
	$$\lim\limits_{x\to -3^-} {2x^2-8 \over x+3} = "{10 \over -0}" = - \infty $$
	\noindent{\bf Task 3. e)}\\
	The pole point found at $x=-3$ is also a vertical asymptote. The numerator degree is one greater than the denominator degree, so we still find a slanted asymptote:
	$$
	\polyset{style=C,div=:,vars=x}
	\polylongdiv{2x^3-2x^2-8x+8}{x^2+2x-3}
	$$
	The slanted asymptote is the term before the remainder:
	$$y=2x-6$$.
