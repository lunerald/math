	%Title
	{\bf Training sheet spherical surface }\\
	\begin{enumerate}[1.]
		
		%Task 1
		{\bf \item Calculate the missing pieces: surface $O$ of a sphere with radius $r$ and diameter}
		$d$. \\
		a) $d=\SI{28}{\milli\meter}$ \hspace{30 pt} b) $ O=\SI{100}{\centi\meter^2}$ \hspace{30 pt} c) $ r=\SI{5.89}{\meter}$
		
		%Exercise 2
		{\bf \item Is there a sphere that has the same measure of volume and surface area?} Justify with calculation!
		
		%Task 3
		{\bf \item Show:} For all spheres, the surface area of the circumscribed cube is the same percentage larger than that of the sphere.
		
		%Task 4
		{\bf \item A sphere has the volume $\SI{5800}{\centi \meter^3}$.} Calculate the surface area and specify the length and width of a rectangle with the same area.
		
		%Task 5
		\begin{minipage}{0.7\textwidth}
			{\bf \item The Reichstag dome is approximately a hemisphere with radius $r=\SI{20}{\meter}$. }
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
				\item Calculate its surface area.
				\item Estimate how thick the glazing is if approx. 240 tons of glass have been installed in it and the density of glass $\varrho_{Glas}= \SI{2.4}{\tonne\per\meter^ 3}$ is.
			\end{enumerate}
		\end{minipage}
		\hfill
		\begin{minipage}{0.25\textwidth}
			\includegraphics[width=4.5 cm]{reichst218}
		\end{minipage}
		
		%Task 6
		{\bf \item A sphere, a hemisphere and a cube each have the same volume $V=\SI{500}{\centi \meter^3}$.} Compare their surface areas as a ratio.
		
		
		%Task 7
		{\bf \item The radius of a balloon increases by 10 \% as it rises into higher air layers.}
		By how much \% does its surface area increase?
		
		%Task 8
		{\bf \item A sample of $\SI{250}{\gram}$ sand is considered. The grain size is $d=\SI{1}{\milli\meter}$, the sand grains are assumed to be round;} the density of the material is $\varrho = \SI{2.5}{\gram \per \centi \meter^3}$.
		\begin{enumerate}[a)]
			\item How long would a row consisting of all the grains in this sample be?
			\item You can use sand, for example, to filter water. What is the combined surface area of all the grains in this sample?
			\item How does the length of the row change if the grain size is doubled?
			\item How does the surface area change when the grain size is doubled?
		\end{enumerate}
		
		%Task 9
		\begin{minipage}{0.7\textwidth}
			{\bf \item Sunspots appear on the surface of the sun every 11 years.} The smallest ones are about the size of the city of Ingolstadt \\$d \approx \SI{15}{\kilo\meter}$, but there were also Sunspots with a diameter of up to $d \approx \SI{150000}{\kilo\meter}$ have already been observed.
			\begin{enumerate}[a)]
				\item By how many powers of ten can the area of the sunspots assumed to be round vary?
				\item Compare the area of this large sunspot with the surface area of the globe $r_E=\SI{6370}{\kilo\meter}$ . What is bigger and how many times?
			\end{enumerate}
		\end{minipage}
		\hfill

		\end{minipage}
