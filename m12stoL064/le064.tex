	\noindent{\bf Task 1. a)}\\
	For $E_1$="Sum  = 2" there are the following options:
	$$E_1=\{20;02;11\}$$
	The probability of a die showing a 2 is $1 \over 6$, for a 1 it is $1 \over 2$, and for a 0 it is $1 \over 3$. So the probability for the sum of the points is 2:
	$$p(E_1)= 2 \cdot {1 \over 6} \cdot {1 \over 3} + \left({1 \over 2}\right)^2={13\over 36}$$

	\noindent{\bf Task 2. a)}\\
	
	The probability for event (i) corresponds to a Bernoulli chain of length 10:
	
	$$P_{1 \over 3}^{10}(X=5)=B(10;{1 \over 3}; 5)={10 \choose 5} \cdot \left( {1 \over 3} \right)^5 \cdot
	\left( {2 \over 3} \right)^5=13.6\%$$
	
	For the probability of event (ii) we transform and read from the table:
	
	$$ P_{1 \over 3}^{10}(4\le X \le 8)= F_{1 \over 3}^{10}(8)-F_{1 \over 3}^{10}( 3)=0.999644-0.559264=44.0\%$$
	
	\noindent {\bf Task 2. b)}
	
	We create the following table of probability distribution:
	
	$$
	\begin{array}{c|c|c|c}
		X_i & -2.90 & 0.10 & 1.10 \\
		\hline
		P(X_i) & {1 \over 3} & {1 \over 6} & {1 \over 2}\\
	\end{array}
	$$
	
	The expected value $E(x)$ of the random variable “profit” is:
	
	$$E(x)= -2.90 \cdot {1 \over 3} + 0.1 \cdot {1 \over 6} + 1.1 \cdot {1 \over 2}=-0.4$$
	
	So you make an average loss of \EUR{0.4} per game.

	\noindent {\bf Task 4.}
	The probability distribution for $Y$
	is:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
	\begin{array}{|c|c|c|c|}
		\hline
		y_i & 1450 & 1900 & 2350 \\
		\hline
		P(Y=y_i)& 0.6 & 0.1 & 0.3 \\
		\hline
	\end{array}
	$$
	The expected value here is:
	$$E(Y)=1450\cdot 0.6 + 1900 \cdot 0.1 +2350 \cdot 0.3 = 1765$$
	The variance is:
	$$Var(Y)=(1450-1765)^2\cdot 0.6 +(1900-1765)^2\cdot 0.1+(2350-1765)^2\cdot 0.3 =164025$$
	So the standard deviation is:
	$$\sigma(Y)=\sqrt{164025}=405$$
