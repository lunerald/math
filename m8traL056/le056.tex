	\noindent{\bf Task 1. a)}\\
	Points $U$ and $L$ have the same y-coordinate. So $[UL]$ is parallel to the x-axis and as long as the difference of the x-coordinates of $U$ and $L$, i.e
	$$\overline{UL}=x_U-x_L=5-0=\SI{5}{\centi\meter}$$
	The height of the trapezoid is the difference of the y coordinates of $U$ and $P$, so
	$$h=y_U -y_P=3-(-2)=5$$
	Because of parallelism, point $A$ must therefore have the same y-coordinate as $P$, so $A(x|-2)$. The length $\overline{PA}$ is therefore the difference of the x-coordinates of $A$ and $P$:
	$$\overline{PA}= x-(-1.5)= x +1.5 \qquad (*)$$
	To determine x we use the general formula for the trapezoidal area, solve it for $\overline{PA}$ and then substitute (*):
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.5}
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		A_{Tr}&=&{1 \over 2}(\overline{PA}+\overline{UL})\cdot h & | \cdot 2 \\
		2\cdot A_{Tr}&=& (\overline{PA}+\overline{UL}) \cdot h & | :H      \\
		{2 \over h} \cdot A_{Tr}&=& \overline{PA}+\overline{UL} & | - \overline{UL} \\
		{2 \over h} \cdot A_{Tr} -\overline{UL} &=& \overline{PA} & \textnormal{insert values}\\
		{2 \over 5}\cdot 30 -5 &=& \overline{PA} & \\
		7 &=& \overline{PA} & (*)\\
		7 &=& x +1.5 & |-1.5\\
		5.5 &=& x
	\end{array}
	$$
	Thus $$A(5,5|-2)$$

	\noindent {\bf task 1. b)}\\
	The parallelogram has the same height again $h=\SI{5}{\centi\meter}$, but the new baseline $[PA']$. The formula for the area is now helpful again for determining x:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.5}
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		A_{P}&=& \overline{PA'} \cdot h & |:h \\
		{A_{P} \over h} &=& \overline{PA'} & (*)\\
		{15 \over 5} &=& x +1.5 \\
		1.5 &=& x & \Rightarrow A'(1.5|-2)
	\end{array}
	$$
	The new parallelogram side is therefore $\SI{3}{\centi\meter}$ long. The point $U'$ is also 3 units of length to the right of L: $$U'(3|3)$$
	
	\noindent {\bf task 1. c)}\\
	The designated area is a triangle with base $g=\SI{1.5}{\centi\meter}$ and height $h=\SI{5}{\centi\meter}$.
	The area is calculated:
	$$A_D={1 \over 2} \, g\cdot h = \SI{3.75}{\centi\meter^2}$$
	Their share of the trapezoid area is:
	$${A_D \over A_{Tr}}=\frac{\SI{3.75}{\centi \meter^2}}{\SI{30}{\centi \meter^2}}={1 \ over 8}$$
