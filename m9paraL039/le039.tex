\noindent{\bf Task 3. e)}\\
A normal parabola that opens upwards has the form:
$$y=x^2 +bx +c$$
We form two equations by substituting the two given points:
$$
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
\begin{array}{rcll}
	-0.75 &=& (-1.5)^2 - 1.5b +c \\
	-3 &=& -1.5b+c &(I)\\
	8 &=& 1 + b + c \\
	7-b &=& c & (II) \\
	II~\textnormal{in}~I\\
	-3 &=& -1.5b +7-b \\
	-10&=& -2.5b \\
	b &=& 4 & c=3
\end{array}
$$
So the equation of this parabola is:
$$y=x^2 +4x+3$$
If the parabola is to belong to the family, $4x=2ax$, i.e. $a=2$ must apply.
The function term would then be:
$$p_2:\quad y= x^2-2\cdot2x +2^2 + 0.5\cdot2 -2 = x^2 -4x + 3$$
$\Rightarrow$ The parabola belongs to the group.

\noindent{\bf Task 4. a)}\\
To determine the vertex we add the square:
$$
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
\begin{array}{rcl}
	y&=& -1.5(x^2-4x)-4.5\\
	&=& -1.5 [(x^2-4x+2^2)-2^2]-4.5 \\
	&=& -1.5[(x-2)^2-4]-4.5\\
	&=& -1.5(x-2)^2+6-4.5 \\
	&=& -1.5(x-2)^2+1.5

$$
The vertex is at $S(2|1,5)$; the set of values is $\mathbb{W}=]-\infty;1.5]$

\noindent{\bf Task 5. a)}\\
When mirroring on the x-axis, we multiply the $y$, i.e. the entire function term, by $-1$ and get:
$$f'(x)=-4(x+1.5)^2+3$$
During a subsequent reflection on the y-axis, we multiply the $x$ contained in the function term by $-1$ and get:
$$f''(x)=-4(-x+1.5)^2+3 \quad \textnormal{or} \quad f''(x)=-4(x-1.5)^2 +3$$

\noindent{\bf Task 6. b)}\\
At the ground $y=0$; We calculate the zeros of the throwing parabola:
$$
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{2}
\begin{array}{rcl}
	-{1 \over 80}(x-80)^2+31.25&=&0 \\
	-{1 \over 80}(x-80)^2 &=& -31.25 \\
	(x-80)^2 &=& 2500 \\
	x-80 &=& \pm 50 \\
	x_1 &=& 130 \\
	(x_2 &=& 30)
\end{array}
$$
The first solution is the x value you are looking for. The arrow flew $130~m-50~m=80~m$.
