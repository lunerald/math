	\noindent {\bf Task 6. a)}\\
	Rectangle side \#1: $x$ ; Rectangle side \#2: $x+6$. The original area is:
	$$A_u = x\cdot(x+6) =x^2+ 6x$$
	The new side lengths are: 1st side: $x+2$; 2nd page: $x+6-3=x+3$
	The new area is therefore:
	$$A_n= (x+2)\cdot(x+3)= x^2+ 5x +6$$
	The new area is $\SI{4}{\centi\meter^2}$ larger, so we equate it to:
	$$
	\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.5}
	\begin{array}{rcll}
		A_n &=& A_u + 4 \\
		x^2+5x+6 &=& x^2 +6x +4 &|-x^2 \\
		5x+6 &=& 6x+4 \\
		2 &=& x
	\end{array}
	$$
	$\Rightarrow $ The original side lengths are: Side 1: $x=\SI{2}{\centi\meter}$
	Side 2: $x+6=\SI{8}{\centi\meter}$
