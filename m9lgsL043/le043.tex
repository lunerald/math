\noindent {\bf Exercise 8.}
We set: $x=$ ones digit $y=$ tens digit. Then the number is $10y + x$, its sum
$x+y$, and we get two equations:
$$
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.5}
\begin{array}{rcll}
	10y + x &=& 3 \cdot (x + y) +22 & (I) \\
	y-1 &=& x & (II) \\
	
\end{array}
$$

(I) can be simplified to:
$$ 7y - 2x = 22 \qquad (I')$$
We substitute (II) into (I') and get:
$$
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.5}
\begin{array}{rcll}
	7y - 2(y-1) &=& 22 & \\
	5y + 2 &=& 22 & |-2 \quad |:5\\
	y &=& 4 &
\end{array}
$$
We insert this result back into (II), so $x=3$. So the number you're looking for is 43.
