		{\bf The Rabbit Problem}
		\vspace{1cm}
		
		The largest possible rectangular rabbit enclosure should be marked out in a meadow by a river. The fence length is $ \SI{20}{\meter}$; There is no fence along the river.
		\vspace{0.5 cm}
		
		\noindent{\bf Step 1:} \\Label the sides of the fence with meaningful variable names.
		\vspace{1.5 cm}
		
	\end{minipage}
	\hfill
	\begin{minipage}{0.4\textwidth}
		\includegraphics[width=8 cm]{kani110}
	\end{minipage}
	\vspace{1cm}
	
	
	\noindent{\bf Step 2:}\\ Create an objective function. Here it is the rectangular area. What is the formula for this?
	\vspace{1.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Step 3:}\\ Formulate a secondary condition. Here it is the fence length. How is it composed? Formulate an equation.
	\vspace{1.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Step 4:}\\ Solve the constraint for a variable.
	\vspace{1.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Step 5:}\\ Insert the solved constraint into the objective function. You then get a function term that only contains one variable.
	\vspace{1.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Step 6:}\\ Multiply the function term and bring it to a square form.
	\vspace{1.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Step 7:}\\We are looking for the extreme value of this term. To do this, carry out a quadratic addition
	\vspace{1.5 cm}
	
	\noindent{\bf Step 8:}\\ Interpret the result.
	\vspace{1.5 cm}
